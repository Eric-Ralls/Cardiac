Myocardial Infarction 1

Myocardial Infarction (MI) is defined as myocardial cell death due to prolonged ischaemia.

Complete necrosis of myocardial cells at risk requires at least 2 to 4 hours, or longer, depending on various factors, such as:

  • Collateral circulation
  • Coronary arterial occlusion
  • Myocyte sensitivity to ischaemia
  • Individual demand for oxygen
